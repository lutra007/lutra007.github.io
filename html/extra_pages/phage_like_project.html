Phage-like plasmid project. Supplementary figure S1

SFigure 1. High synteny between phage-like plasmids

All grouped phages and phage-like plasmids are presented as linear maps (left) and on the dotplots (right). Group references are shown as the upper sequences on the genetic maps and the dotplots. Groups are divided into subgroups of convinient size by the major replicons.

(left) Sequences are labeled with their database ID, GenBank ID and bacterial host. Annotation is shown for the reference and for the functional group discussed in the main text, ARGs, replication regions, IS elements, virulence factors, toxin/antitoxin, tRNA sequences, and conjugation genes. Legend is fixed on the right.
No extra symbols – 100% gene coverage and 100% identity; # – ≥60% query coverage and ≥90% nucleotide identity.
Alignment results are shown for the neighboring sequences: pink – alignment in the same direction, blue – in the opposite direction, where brightness represent an identity between two aligned regions.

(right) Dotplots of all-to-all comparisons. Sequences with blocks homologous to the reference, but organized in the opposite direction are labeled on the Y-axis.
